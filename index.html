About me

Welcome! My name is Seungjun Kim and I am an Assistant Professor at KDI School of Public Policy and Management. My research examines the intersection of international political economy and foreign direct investment, with particular emphasis on government-business relationships, industrial policies, and geo-economic statecraft.

My work contributes to understanding how these dynamics affect the liberal international order amid intensifying geo-economic competition. I am particularly interested in how states use economic tools to achieve strategic objectives and how multinational corporations navigate increasingly complex geo-economic landscapes.

Teaching

International Political Economy

Graduate Course • Fall 2024

This graduate seminar examines the intersection of politics and economics in the international system, covering trade, finance, development, and global governance institutions. Students engage with both theoretical frameworks and contemporary policy challenges.

Global Economic Policy

Undergraduate Course • Spring 2024

An introduction to major economic policy issues in the global economy, including international trade, monetary systems, development strategies, and economic integration. Emphasis on policy analysis and real-world applications.

Foreign Direct Investment and Development

Graduate Course • Fall 2023

Advanced seminar examining the role of foreign direct investment in economic development, covering theoretical perspectives, empirical evidence, and policy implications for developing and developed countries.

Research Methods in Political Economy

Graduate Course • Spring 2023

Introduction to quantitative and qualitative research methods in political economy, including research design, data collection, statistical analysis, and academic writing. Hands-on training with statistical software.
